Service Design Methodology

Great products are built on a deep understanding of the entire customer experience. I use service design to create a holistic view of how customers interact with your business, ensuring every touchpoint is intentional, consistent, and valuable.

My process involves creating detailed customer journey maps and service blueprints. This visualizes the entire ecosystem—from the user's initial awareness to long-term loyalty—highlighting moments of frustration and opportunities for delight. By addressing the front-stage (what customers see) and back-stage (internal processes), we can build a truly seamless experience.

Potential Business Impact:

Up to 30% reduction in customer support tickets by proactively solving user issues.
Increase customer retention and LTV by designing for loyalty and satisfaction.
Improve Net Promoter Score (NPS) by delivering a consistently positive experience.
Streamline operations by aligning back-stage processes with front-stage user actions.

Case Study: Redesigning a B2B SaaS Platform

Client: TechCorp Inc., a mid-size enterprise software provider.

Challenge: The client's flagship product suffered from low user engagement and high churn rates due to a complex and unintuitive interface. They needed to create a more user-friendly experience without disrupting existing customer workflows.

Outcome: Through comprehensive service mapping and user journey analysis, we redesigned the entire user experience. The project resulted in a 40% increase in user satisfaction scores, a 25% reduction in onboarding time, and a significant decrease in customer churn within six months of launch.

Product Strategy & Roadmapping

A winning product needs a clear vision and a strategic path to achieve it. I work with leadership teams to define a product strategy that aligns with your business objectives, finds a strong market fit, and sets you apart from the competition.

This process starts with deep market and competitive analysis to identify unique opportunities. From there, we build a clear product vision and a prioritized, theme-based roadmap. This roadmap isn't just a list of features; it's a strategic communication tool that outlines how we'll deliver value to customers over time, enabling your team to stay focused and aligned.

Potential Business Impact:

Up to 20% faster time-to-market by focusing on high-impact initiatives.
Increase revenue from new features by solving real customer problems.
Improve stakeholder confidence and team alignment with a clear, strategic roadmap.
Enhance market positioning by focusing on unique value propositions.

Case Study: Launching a New Mobile App

Client: ConnectApp, a startup in the social networking space.

Challenge: The client had a validated idea but needed a clear strategy to enter a crowded market. They required a product roadmap that focused on a minimum viable product (MVP) to test their core hypothesis quickly.

Outcome: We developed a phased product strategy that prioritized key differentiators. The resulting roadmap allowed the team to launch their MVP 3 months ahead of initial estimates, securing a crucial round of seed funding based on strong early adoption metrics.

From one-on-one interviews and contextual inquiries to surveys and usability testing, I gather both qualitative and quantitative data. These insights are synthesized into actionable recommendations, personas, and user stories that empower your team to make informed, user-centered decisions at every stage of the product development lifecycle.

Potential Business Impact:

Up to 50% increase in user task completion rates through improved usability.
Reduce development rework by validating ideas and designs before writing code.
Boost adoption and conversion by building features users genuinely need.
Drive innovation with a deep understanding of unmet user needs.

Challenge: The company wanted to invest in a major new feature but was unsure of its market viability. They needed to validate the core concept with real users before committing significant development resources.

Outcome: We conducted a series of in-depth user interviews and prototype tests. The research revealed critical insights that led to a major pivot in the feature's direction, saving an estimated $200,000 in development costs by avoiding building the wrong solution.
